Machine Learning Reproducibility Fellow at UC Santa Cruz Summer Of Reproducibility . Worked on the project, "Using Reproducibility in Machine Learning Education," where I tackled the challenge of reproducibility in academic research, particularly focusing on the domain of machine learning education. This initiative centered around the application of Few-Shot Learning techniques using BERT for Intent Classification. A significant aspect of my work was to demonstrate the surprisingly minimal impact of synonym-based text augmentation on the model's performance. Additionally, I developed comprehensive educational resources aimed at illustrating the critical issue of incomplete methodology descriptions in academic publications. By highlighting how such gaps can lead to divergent interpretative choices during the reproduction of research, my work shed light on the consequential effects on result validation. This project not only contributes to the field of machine learning by providing a practical examination of reproducibility challenges but also serves as a valuable educational tool that underscores the importance of meticulous methodological reporting for the integrity of scientific research.
